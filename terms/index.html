Welcome to Pafly. By using our application, you agree to these Terms of Service. Please read them carefully.

1. About Pafly

Pafly is a mobile application for anonymous message exchange, designed as a daily writing ritual. Each user can write a short, free-form message that will later be read by a stranger. The identity of both the sender and the recipient is never revealed.

Pafly is not a social network, a messaging app, a public feed, or a real-time chat. It is an experience built on slowness, anonymity, fairness, and sincerity.

2. User Accounts

You can use Pafly without creating an account. By default, users connect via anonymous authentication. No real identity is required to start using the app.

3. The Core Principle

Pafly works like a bottle on the water. You write a message and send it into the world. Meanwhile, messages from others may arrive at any time — no action is required to receive one. No message is artificially generated. Waiting time may vary depending on global activity. Waiting and uncertainty are part of the experience.

4. Messages and Content

You may write a maximum of one message per day. Messages are free in tone and content, as long as they comply with our moderation rules.

Messages may be up to 500 characters. All messages must contain at least 50 characters.

By submitting a message, you grant Pafly a non-exclusive, worldwide license to store, process, moderate, and deliver your message to another user within the application. You retain ownership of your content.

5. Content Moderation

Every message is reviewed before being delivered. Moderation is handled by AI-powered content moderation. Offensive, hateful, violent, discriminatory, or illegal content is blocked and will not be distributed.

We reserve the right to block, filter, or remove any content that violates these terms or applicable law, at our sole discretion.

6. Reactions and Replies

Users can react to received messages with any of the available reaction types. Only one reaction per message per user is allowed. All reactions are anonymous.

Users may send a short text reply (up to 280 characters) to received standalone messages. One reply per message is allowed.
